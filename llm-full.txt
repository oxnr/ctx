CTX POSITIONING
- `ctx` is an AI operating architecture practice with a public learning layer.
- It is not a product company and not just a content site. It combines public education, repo-native operating artifacts, and engagement-ready delivery patterns.
- The public site currently has four primary surfaces:
  - `/` = Value flow in AI — value-chain map, explorer, and provider landscape snapshot
  - `/thesis` = thesis page
  - `/learn` = academy mapped to the value chain
  - `/glossary` = shared vocabulary
- `/practice`, `/stack`, and `/value-chain` are compatibility routes in the current iteration and may redirect.
- The repo is meant to stay legible to both humans and agents: plain text first, explicit scripts, minimal hidden behavior.

THESIS
- Velocity is the constant. New models drop every other month and code ships at record velocity. The durable advantage is the ability to absorb change without rebuilding.
- Build for change. Providers, models, prompts, workflows, processes, and team roles should all be designed to evolve — not just code.
- Harness is the real asset. The system around the model — context, workflows, evaluation, integrations, and decisions — is the expensive part. Context sits at the center, but the harness is broader.
- New paradigms. Local-first, API-first, SDK-first, agents, tool use, and structured outputs are not trends to watch — they are paradigms to adopt.
- Human in the loop. Humans own decisions that are irreversible, high-stakes, or ambiguous. The boundary between human judgment and machine execution should be explicit, not emergent.

VALUE-CHAIN MODEL

- Many entities span multiple layers; do not force false precision when categorizing tools or companies.

- Keep public claims sourceable, dated, and easy to refresh.

REPEATED OPERATING LOOP
1. State intent and constraints in one short brief.
2. Define one narrow deliverable with one acceptance check.
3. Execute the relevant skill or script with complete context.
4. Capture validation artifacts, assumptions, and risks.
5. Fix the root cause, not only the symptom.
6. Update canonical docs or decisions before closing.
7. Add one cleanup action that reduces future ambiguity.
